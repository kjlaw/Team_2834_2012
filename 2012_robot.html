Current Season's Robot

The day after kickoff, we were all so excited and full of ideas. What capabilities should our robot have? How can we all agree on one design? One of our mentors introduced us to a planning and consensus tool called Quality Function Deployment (QFD). After we went through the process, we learned that the most important features our robot should possess ought to be the ability to cross the barrier and the bridge, the ability to collect balls off the ground and from the inbounding station, and the ability to shoot accurately from any robot field orientation.

Chassis
The robot uses a two speed transmission system which allows high torque for pushing in low gear and quick movement in high gear. The low gear also helps in climbing on to the bridge and crossing the barrier. A bridge lowering device extends out in the front of the robot, activated by pneumatic cylinders.

Ball Collection System
Powered rollers are used to collect balls off the ground as the robot drives over them. The robot is wide and short to allow for a bigger opening in the front, which makes collecting balls easier. The robot can also collect balls from the inbounding station directly without having the ball drop to the ground first. This will eliminate the chance of losing the ball to the opposing alliance during the process.

Ball Shooting System
To allow the robot to shoot balls quickly without having to orient the robot to face the right direction, we designed a 360 degree turret system with a camera mounted to it. The shooter uses two wheels on the bottom which help center the ball and create the maximum back spin. There is also an adjustable hood to change the ball departure angle based on the robot's distance from the rim.
